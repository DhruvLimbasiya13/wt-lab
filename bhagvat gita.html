About

What is the Bhagavad Gita?

The Bhagavad Gita (“Song of the Divine”) is a spiritual classic where Krishna teaches Arjuna the paths of selfless action, devotion, and knowledge, resolving the dilemma of how to act in a complex world.

Context & Setting

On the eve of battle, Arjuna questions duty and morality; Krishna responds with universal teachings.

Core Aim

To act wisely and selflessly, seeing the same Self in all beings.

Key Themes

Four Yogas Highlighted

Different paths, one goal — inner freedom and union with the Divine.

Karma Yoga

The yoga of selfless action. Perform duty without attachment to results.

Jnana Yoga

The yoga of knowledge. Discern the eternal Self from the changing world.

Bhakti Yoga

The yoga of devotion. Offer all actions and outcomes to the Divine.

Dhyana Yoga

The yoga of meditation. Steady the mind and abide in inner stillness.
